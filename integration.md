## Introduction

Multisensory/multimodal perception is how animals form coherent, valid, and robust **perception** by processing sensory **stimuli** from various modalities.

**Perception** way that sensory information is interpreted and consciously experienced  
**Stimuli** (singular: stimulus) are any detectable change in the internal or external environment.  
**Sensation** occures when sensory receptors in the body detect stimuli and convert them into neural signals that are sent to the brain for processing.

Not all sensations result in perception
---

## Stimuli and Sensory Modalities
There are four attributes of stimulus: modality, intensity, location, and duration.

## Principles of Multisensory Integration 
- **Temporal principle**: stimuli from different modalities are more likely to be integrated if they occur at the same time.
- **Spatial principle**: stimuli from different modalities are more likely to be integrated if they occur
at the same location.
- **Inverse effectiveness principle**: stimuli from different modalities are more likely to be integrated if they are weakly effective when presented alone.

## Multisensory Illusions
- **McGurk effect**: a perceptual phenomenon that occurs when auditory and visual components of a speech signal are mismatched, leading to a third, distinct perception.
- **Ventriloquist effect**: a phenomenon where the perceived location of a sound is influenced by visual cues, such as the movement of a puppet's mouth.
- **Rubber hand illusion**: a phenomenon where a person experiences ownership of a fake rubber hand when it is stroked synchronously with their own hidden hand.
